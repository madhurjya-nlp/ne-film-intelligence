National Overseas Scholarship for ST

Ministry of Tribal Affairs · Govt. of India
Your single most powerful tool. As an ST candidate from Assam, you are eligible for full government funding to study abroad.

Covers: Tuition (actual) + Maintenance ($15,400/yr) + Contingency ($1,532) + Visa, medical insurance, airfare
Eligibility: Max age 32 (you're 25 ✓) · Family income ≤ ₹6L/yr · University must be in QS Top 1000
Min marks: 55% in Bachelor's · Slots: 20 awards/yr (17 ST + 3 PVTG)
Bond: None after graduation
₹0 fully funded →

🇪🇪 Baltic States — Estonia · Latvia · Lithuania

Strong film traditions, part of the Nordic-Baltic film network, affordable education (€2,000-8,000/yr for non-EU), English-taught programs, and directly connected to Erasmus Mundus film consortia.

Tallinn University (BFM)

MA in Documentary Film / Cross-Media Production
BFM is one of Europe's most respected film schools. English-taught. Tuition: ~€2,500-4,000/yr. Check for hybrid thesis tracks. Estonian government scholarships available via DreamApply platform.
~₹2.3-3.8L/yr · Scholarships available →

Latvian Academy of Culture

MA in Film & Audiovisual Arts
National Film School, deeply integrated into Baltic/Nordic film network. Latvian State Scholarships available for international students (can cover tuition + living). English-taught.
State scholarships available →

Lithuanian Academy of Music & Theatre

MA in Film · National Film School
Part of FILMEU European University Alliance and the DocNomads consortium. Practical filmmaking focus. Affordable tuition for non-EU students.
Part of Erasmus network →

Major hub for KinoEyes & DocNomads consortia. On-campus programs at €500-5,000/yr. Portugal offers a vibrant film culture with strong ties to Lusophone Africa and Brazil.

🇨🇿 Central Europe — Czech · Poland · Hungary · Romania

Home to legendary film schools (FAMU, Łódź). Romania's Sapientia has possibly the cheapest film MA in Europe at €550/year.

€550/yr — ~₹47K!

Sapientia Hungarian University

MA in Film Studies · Romania · 2 years
Joint with ELTE Budapest. €550/year (~₹47,000/yr!) — incredibly cheap. 10 tuition-free spots available. Admission by oral exam. On-campus but worth contacting about research-based remote thesis options.
₹47K/yr · 10 free spots →

FAMU — Academy of Performing Arts

MA in Film & TV · Czech Republic
World-famous film school. On-campus only. English-taught programs. Tuition: €4,000-8,000/yr. Prague is a major European film hub with strong indie scene.
€4,000-8,000/yr →

Łódź Film School

Various MA programs · Poland
One of the world's oldest and most prestigious film schools. On-campus. Low tuition by European standards. Strong alumni network including Wajda, Kieślowski, Polanski.
Low tuition · On-campus →

🇨🇺 Cuba — EICTV

World-renowned "learning by doing" philosophy. Founded by Gabriel García Márquez. Online workshops (€500-1,200 = ₹42,000-1L) are within budget and carry enormous prestige in global independent cinema circles.

Online Workshops Available

EICTV — Escuela Internacional de Cine y TV

Online workshops · 3-year Regular Course · Cuba
Legendary film school. Online workshops: €500-1,200 (~₹42K-1L) — within budget. Multicultural, prestigious alumni network. The 3-year regular course is the gold standard for Latin American film education. Spanish required for most programs.
€500-1,200 workshops →
